HISTORY OF CENTRAL PHILIPPINES STATE UNIVERSITY


The colorful history of CENTRAL PHILIPPINES STATE UNIVERSITY started when House Bill Number 396 introduced by Honorable Congressman Carlos Hilado, and co-authored by congressmen Limsiaco, Medina, Formilleza, Nietes and Arnaldo for the purpose of establishing one agricultural school in Negros Occidental of the Central Luzon Agricultural School-type. One of the immediate missions of the school was to hasten economic development of the country which was in the process of reconstruction by the training of the youth in scientific agriculture . House Bill 396 had ripened into Republic Act Number 43 that officially created the Negros Occidental National Agricultural School (NONAS) on October 2, 1946. It became operational on August 1of the following year. NONAS has served the pioneer ing students for 31 long years from 1946-1977. As time passed by, NONAS attained another milestone of maturity. By virtue of the Presidential Authority on September 6, 1977, NONAS was converted into Negros Occidental Agricultural College. (NOAC). NOAC has steadfastly turned out both College and High School graduates for 24 years prior to its conversion into a State College. NOAC then was converted into a Stat e College known as the CENTRAL PHILIPPINES STATE UNIVERSITY (CPSU) by virtue of Republic Act 9141, dated July 3, 2001 sponsored by Congressman Generao “Lim-ao” Alvarez, Jr. By law, it is meant to open extension campuses in all cities and muni cipalities within the sixth district of Negros Occidental. CPSU sprawls in 4,653.7 hectares situated in the heart of Negros island, 20 kilometer from City of Kabankalan and 17 kilometers from adjacent town of Mabinay, Negros Oriental. The main campus is tra versed by an all-whether national highway and accessible to regular busses and jeepneys making track Bacolod via Kabankalan City through Dumaguete City the capital city of Oriental Negros.
